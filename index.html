Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

The Quota Reform Movement in Bangladesh, which took place in 2018, was driven by students demanding reform of the country's public sector job quota system. This system reserved 56% of government jobs for various groups, which the students found unjust, calling for merit-based recruitment.

During the movement, there were instances of clashes between protestors and law enforcement, leading to injuries among students. Various organizations, human rights groups, and individuals expressed solidarity and provided support, including medical aid, to those injured.
